Research and publications

Modeling transport across scales

I study interfacial transport, reactor-scale CFD, and polymer mechanics using physics-based and data-driven modeling. Highlights below summarize current projects and recent outputs.

Research projects

Fluid-repelling surfaces and interfacial slip

  • Modeled nanoscale slip and water-repelling surfaces with LAMMPS and continuum theory.
  • Built physics-based predictors of hydrophobicity with >99% accuracy.
  • Scaled analysis with a 25,000+ simulation pipeline and automated post-processing.

Carbon capture absorber optimization

  • Simulated absorber columns with COMSOL for energy-efficient carbon adsorption.
  • Automated CAD generation, CFD runs, and analysis to accelerate design cycles.
  • Developed topology insights that balance catalyst exposure and pumping cost.

Self-healing polymers for sustainable packaging

  • Modeled polymer stiffness and crystallinity from microstructural properties.
  • Validated experimental trends with molecular simulations and in-silico synthesis.
  • Generated 200+ polymers using Python and Bash automation.

Microreactor transport and mixing

  • Developed 2D/3D CFD models to diagnose mixing bottlenecks.
  • Derived optimal flow conditions to improve conversion and reduce reactant loss.
  • Recognized with a poster award in a 60-poster symposium.

Fe-TAML catalyst transport for water treatment

  • Built quantum-chemistry models of Fe-TAML interactions in water.
  • Explored surface chemistry effects across 25+ unique geometries.
  • Executed 500+ simulations in LAMMPS to support catalyst design.
